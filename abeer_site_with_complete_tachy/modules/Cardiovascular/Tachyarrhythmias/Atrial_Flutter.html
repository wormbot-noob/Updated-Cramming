Atrial Flutter

📌 Definition

Atrial flutter is a macro-reentrant tachyarrhythmia, typically from the right atrium, with an atrial rate of 250–350 bpm. ECG shows a “saw-tooth” flutter wave pattern, especially in leads II, III, aVF.

🧠 Pathophysiology

  • Caused by a large reentrant circuit, most often in the right atrium
  • Counterclockwise rotation in typical flutter (CTI-dependent)
  • Often alternates with atrial fibrillation

🩺 Clinical Features

  • Rapid, regular palpitations or fatigue
  • May feel “fluttering” in chest
  • Pulse may be regular (e.g. 150 bpm) with 2:1 AV block

🔍 Diagnosis

  • ECG: Sawtooth flutter waves without isoelectric baseline
  • Inferior leads best for visualizing flutter waves
  • AV nodal blockers or vagal maneuvers can reveal hidden waves

🧭 Management

  • Anticoagulation: Same as AF based on CHA₂DS₂-VASc
  • Rate control: Beta-blockers, diltiazem
  • Rhythm control: Electrical cardioversion highly effective
  • Ablation: CTI ablation is often curative (>95% success)

⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Misdiagnosing 2:1 flutter as sinus tachycardia (~150 bpm)
  • Under-recognizing embolic risk – same stroke prevention as AF
  • Assuming rate control is sufficient – rhythm control may be needed
