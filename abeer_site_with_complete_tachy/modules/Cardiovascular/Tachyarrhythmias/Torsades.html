Torsades de Pointes (TdP)

📌 Definition

TdP is a polymorphic ventricular tachycardia that occurs in the setting of a prolonged QT interval. The QRS complexes twist around the baseline in a sinusoidal fashion, often self-terminating or degenerating into VF.

🧠 Pathophysiology

  • Triggered by early afterdepolarizations (EADs)
  • QT prolongation from drugs, electrolyte disturbances, or congenital syndromes
  • Pause-dependent: often initiated by a PVC following a long pause (“R on T”)

🩺 Clinical Features

  • Palpitations, syncope, seizures, or sudden death
  • Often seen in hospitalized patients on multiple QT-prolonging drugs

🔍 Diagnosis

  • ECG: Long QT followed by polymorphic VT with twisting axis
  • Measure QTc (>500 ms is high risk)
  • Look for culprit medications or low K/Mg

🧭 Management

  • First-line: IV magnesium sulfate (even if Mg is normal)
  • Temporary pacing or isoproterenol to shorten QT if Mg fails
  • Remove offending agents and correct electrolytes (K > 4, Mg > 2)
  • Beta blockers + ICD for congenital long QT syndromes

⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to give magnesium — it's the first-line treatment
  • Treating like monomorphic VT (amiodarone may worsen it)
  • Not addressing underlying causes: QT-prolonging drugs or hypokalemia
