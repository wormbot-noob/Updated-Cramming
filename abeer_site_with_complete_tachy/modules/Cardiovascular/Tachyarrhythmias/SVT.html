Paroxysmal Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)

📌 Definition

SVT refers to any rapid, regular rhythm that originates above the ventricles. Most common forms include AVNRT and AVRT. It typically presents with sudden onset and termination, and heart rates between 150–250 bpm.

🧠 Pathophysiology

  • AVNRT: Micro-reentry within the AV node (dual pathways)
  • AVRT: Macro-reentry involving accessory pathway (e.g. WPW)
  • Triggered by premature beats and autonomic changes

🩺 Clinical Features

  • Sudden onset palpitations, anxiety, dyspnea, chest discomfort
  • Common in young women
  • May terminate spontaneously or with vagal maneuvers

🔍 Diagnosis

  • Regular narrow-complex tachycardia (QRS < 120 ms)
  • P waves may be hidden or retrograde
  • Capture during episode on ECG or Holter monitor

🧭 Management

  • Stable: Vagal maneuvers → IV adenosine → AV nodal blockers (BB/CCB)
  • Unstable: Immediate synchronized cardioversion
  • Recurrent: Consider EP study and catheter ablation

⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Misdiagnosing SVT as panic attacks
  • Giving diltiazem/verapamil to wide complex tachycardia (may be VT)
  • Missing WPW: Avoid AV nodal blockers in AF + WPW
