Atrial Fibrillation (AF)

📌 Definition

AF is the most common sustained arrhythmia, marked by disorganized atrial activity and no effective atrial contraction. ECG: irregularly irregular rhythm without P waves.

📊 ECG Appearance

▶️ Simulate AF on EKGmon

🧠 Pathophysiology

  • Ectopic foci from pulmonary veins
  • Reentrant circuits maintained in a dilated atrium
  • Risk factors: HTN, heart failure, valvular disease, hyperthyroidism

🩺 Clinical Features

  • Palpitations, fatigue, dizziness, dyspnea, or asymptomatic
  • Irregularly irregular pulse

🔍 Diagnosis

  • ECG confirms: No P waves, irregular R-R intervals
  • Investigate causes: TSH, electrolytes, alcohol, infection

🧭 Management (ABC Pathway)

  • A: Anticoagulation (CHA₂DS₂-VASc)
  • B: Better symptom control (rate/rhythm)
  • C: Comorbidity management (HTN, OSA, etc.)

🧪 Treatment

  • Rate control: β-blockers, diltiazem, digoxin
  • Rhythm control: Cardioversion, ablation, antiarrhythmics
  • Anticoagulation: Based on CHA₂DS₂-VASc score

⚠️ Common Pitfalls

  • Cardioversion without anticoagulation (AF >48h)
  • Using AV nodal blockers in AF with WPW
  • Forgetting to assess for hyperthyroidism or infection
